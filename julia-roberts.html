All you need to know about Julia Roberts

Julia Roberts is a highly regarded American actress with a net worth of $250 million. Julia Roberts has long been one of the highest-paid celebrities in the world. Between film pay and sponsorships, she easily makes $20-$40 million every year.

Who is Julia Roberts?

Julia Roberts was born in Smyrna, Georgia on October 28, 1967. Her parents, Betty Lou Bredemus and Walter Grady Roberts, met while starring in military theater shows and co-founded the Atlanta Actors and Writers Workshop. They also managed a children’s acting school in Decatur, Georgia, where they taught the children of Martin Luther King, Jr. and Coretta Scott King. After her parents’ divorce was formalized in early 1972, Roberts relocated to Smyrna, Georgia, where she later graduated from Campbell High School.

Following graduation, she briefly attended Georgia State University before relocating to New York City to pursue an acting career. After arriving in the city, she joined Click Modeling Agency and enrolled in acting school. Julia’s older brother, Eric Roberts, and niece, Emma Roberts, are also actresses, however, she was estranged from Eric for several years before 2004.

After appearing in supporting or co-starring parts in films such as “Mystic Pizza” and “Satisfaction,” she rose to prominence with her Oscar-nominated performance in “Steel Magnolias.” Then, in 1990, she became a tremendous, breakthrough star. Her appearance opposite Richard Gere in “Pretty Woman” launched her career, and for the next decade, she appeared in a new hit movie every year.

Between 1990 and 2000, she starred in 21 films, including “Flatliners,” “Hook,” “Dying Young,” “Sleeping with the Enemy,” “The Pelican Brief,” “Michael Collins,” “Conspiracy Theory,” “My Best Friend’s Wedding,” “Runaway Bride,” “Notting Hill,” and “Erin Brokovich,” for which she received an Oscar. She has also appeared in “Ocean’s Eleven” and its sequels, “Confessions of a Dangerous Mind”, “Mona Lisa Smile”, “Closer”, “Fireflies in the Garden”, and “Eat Pray Love”.

What is Julia Roberts’s career?

Julia Roberts made her debut big-screen appearance in the 1988 picture “Satisfaction,” which featured Liam Neeson and Justine Bateman. Her other early ventures include an appearance in an episode of the series “Crime Story” in February 1987, the independent film “Mystic Pizza” (1988), and a supporting role in “Steel Magnolias” (1989). Her work in “Steel Magnolias” earned her her first Academy Award nomination (for Best Supporting Actress) and first Golden Globe award (for Motion Picture Best Supporting Actress).

Her breakthrough role occurred in 1990 when she starred opposite Richard Gere in “Pretty Woman.” Her performance as an assertive freelance hooker garnered her a second Oscar nomination, this time for Best Actress, as well as her second Golden Globe award for Motion Picture Best Actress (Musical or Comedy). “Pretty Woman” had the best ticket sales in the United States at the time for a romantic comedy, grossing $463.4 million worldwide.

She received a $300,000 compensation for the film. Other important movies she worked on in the 1990s include “Flatliners” (1990), “Sleeping with the Enemy” (1991), “The Pelican Brief” (1993), “My Best Friend’s Wedding” (1997), and “Notting Hill” (1999).

In the film “Erin Brockovich” (2000), Roberts played real-life environmental activist Erin Brockovich and earned $20 million for her role. The film was a critical and commercial success, grossing $256.3 million worldwide, and Roberts received the Academy Award for Best Actress for her performance. Because of her A-list reputation, she was one of the highest-paid actresses of the 1990s, and she was the first actress to appear on The Hollywood Reporter’s list of the 50 most influential women in show business since its inception in 1992.

Julia Roberts, one of Hollywood’s biggest performers, has earned impressive earnings throughout her storied career, indicating her financial stability and celebrity power. Beginning with her early parts, Roberts received $50,000 for “Mystic Pizza” and increased significantly to $300,000 for her legendary role in “Pretty Woman.” She was paid $90,000 for “Steel Magnolias,” and $500,000 for “Flatliners.”

Although she received a scale payment of only $4,000 for a “Law & Order” appearance, her film performances in “Sleeping with the Enemy” and “Dying Young” earned her $1 million and $3 million respectively.
