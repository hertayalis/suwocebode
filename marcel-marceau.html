Biography

Marcel Marceau was born in Strasbourg, France on March 22, 1923. Legendary French mime who raised pantomime to an expressive art form. He is best known for his stage persona as “Bip the Clown” and he was awarded the National Order of Merit in France in 1998. He created the character Bip the Clown, who became his alter ego like “Little Tramp” became Charlie Chaplin‘s alter ego. During World War II, he was a member of the French Resistance and saved dozens of children from race laws and concentration camps. He gave his first major performance to 3000 troops after the liberation of Paris in August 1944.
