Michael Ibo Cooper died at 71 : The Reggae Maestros Final Bow

The world of reggae music was left in mourning with the news of the death of Michael “Ibo” Cooper, an iconic Jamaican musician and musical educator. He passed away on October 12, 2023, at the age of 71.

About Michael ibo Cooper

Michael “Ibo” Cooper, born January 14th 1952, was a revered figure within the reggae community. He was a member of the popular band Inner Circle and a founding member of the reggae band Third World. He was also the husband of Joy Cooper. His contributions to the genre and his passion for musical education have left a lasting impact.

Achievement and Career

Cooper’s career spanned over two decades, during which he played a pivotal role in the global dissemination of reggae music. After 25 years on the road, Cooper transitioned into a new phase of his life as a teacher and advocate for reggae and popular music in Kingston. His work as an educator further solidified his legacy within the industry.

How Did Michael Cooper Die?

Cooper passed away on Thursday, October 12, 20231. The news of his death was reported by several sources, including the Jamaica Observer and Loop Jamaica.

Cause of Death & Tributes

While the exact cause of Cooper’s death has not been explicitly stated, it was reported he died after a shor illness. Tributes have poured in from around the world, with many fans, colleagues, and students expressing their sorrow and sharing their memories of the legendary musician.

The passing of Michael “Ibo” Cooper marks the end of an era in reggae music. His indelible influence on the genre and his dedication to musical education will continue to inspire future generations.
